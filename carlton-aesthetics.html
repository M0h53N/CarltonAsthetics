Our Standards

The questions you should ask any clinic — answered

Choosing an injector is a medical decision. Here is exactly where we stand on the things that matter.

Who will treat me?

Every treatment is carried out personally by a pharmacist registered with the General Pharmaceutical Council (GPhC), with accredited training in facial aesthetics — never a delegate, never a technician.

Are you insured?

Yes — we hold comprehensive medical indemnity insurance covering every treatment we offer. Documentation is available to view at your consultation.

Is the clinic hygienic?

Treatments take place in a dedicated clinical room within our pharmacy, following strict medical-grade hygiene and single-use protocols — the same standards we've upheld as a healthcare provider for years.

What products do you use?

Only leading, licensed injectables sourced through regulated pharmaceutical channels — the benefit of being based within a pharmacy. We will always tell you exactly what is being used, and why.

Common Questions

Before you book

Will my results look natural?

That is the entire philosophy of the clinic. We treat conservatively, build gradually, and would rather you return for a little more than ever leave over-treated. Nobody should be able to tell you've had anything done — only that you look well.

Does it hurt?

Discomfort is minimal. We use ultra-fine needles and, for filler treatments, products containing local anaesthetic. Most clients describe it as far easier than expected.

How long do results last?

Anti-wrinkle results typically last 3–4 months; dermal fillers from 6 to 18 months depending on the area and product. We'll give you honest, personalised timelines at consultation.

Why does treatment require a consultation first?

Anti-wrinkle injections are prescription-only medicines, so a face-to-face medical consultation is a legal and clinical requirement — and it's also how we make sure any treatment is genuinely right for you. Consultations carry no obligation.

What if something isn't right for me?

Then we'll tell you, plainly. As healthcare professionals our first duty is to you, not to a sale. Sometimes the best advice is skincare, patience, or nothing at all.
